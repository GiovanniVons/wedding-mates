## Why move off $950

Two reasons, and they both cost you money as it stands.

**1. $950 makes people compare you to a celebrant, and you lose that comparison.**
A regular celebrant in Australia charges roughly $600 to $1,200 and does the whole thing for the couple. At $950 you sit right inside that range, so a couple reads you as "a slightly dearer celebrant who also makes us do homework." That is the wrong fight. You are not a cheaper celebrant. You are the only way a couple's own brother, sister or best mate can stand up and lead the ceremony, with the legal side fully handled. Once the price clears about $1,200, people stop filing you next to celebrants and start judging you on what you actually are, where you have no competitor.

**2. The price is part of how couples decide whether to trust you.**
This is the one wedding moment that cannot be redone. The couple's real worry is not "is this too dear," it is "will my mate freeze up on the day." When you have no reviews yet, the price is one of the few signals you have. A low price reads as light and untested and quietly makes that worry worse. A confident price says "this is a real program with a registered celebrant behind it." Pricing up here actually calms the nerves rather than adding to them.

Think of it the way couples already think about a wedding: they happily spend $4,000 on a photographer and $3,000 on a video to capture the ceremony. This makes the ceremony itself the one they actually wanted, led by someone who loves them. Against a wedding that already costs thirty to fifty thousand dollars, the difference between $950 and $1,490 is not the thing that decides the sale.

## The most important change: stop charging extra for the safety net

Until now, the **Zoom rehearsal ($99)** and the **script review ($129)** were sold as optional add-ons. Those two things are exactly what stop a nervous first-timer from falling apart on the day. Selling them separately means the couples who most need them are the ones most likely to skip them.

So we have built both **into The Ceremony, Complete**. You are selling "your mate will be brilliant on the day," and now the two things that make that true are included, not upsold. This is the single biggest reason we expect Complete to be the package most couples choose, and the reason the whole offer feels more trustworthy.

## Two things we recommend you add (your call, not ours to build alone)

These are not code changes. They are commitments only you can make, and they would each lift conversion noticeably.

1. **A "your mate will not freeze" promise.** Something like: *if your mate is not feeling confident after the rehearsal, Sarah steps in to help lead, at no extra cost.* This is the most powerful thing you can offer, because it removes the couple's single biggest fear. We have left it out of the live site until you are comfortable committing to it, since it is your time on the line. If you say yes, we add it to The Ceremony, Complete.

2. **Lead with your own credentials.** You are a real Commonwealth-registered celebrant who trained to marry your own best friend. For a service like this, that is stronger proof than any wall of testimonials, especially while the reviews are still coming in. We would put that front and centre.
